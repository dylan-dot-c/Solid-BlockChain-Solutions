Here is a list of frequenly asked questions

What is blockchain?
a system in which a record of transactions made in bitcoin or another cryptocurrency are maintained across several computers that are linked in a peer-to-peer network.
How can Jamaica Benefit from blockChain?
There are many benefits for Jamaica and the BlockChain technology some of these include, Election, Real Estate, Banking and Transportation.
What is blockchain vs crypto?
A cryptocurrency is a form of digital money. Bitcoin, Ether, Litecoin, and Tether are examples. Units of cryptocurrency are called coins or tokens. A blockchain is a distributed peer-to-peer database that has strict rules for adding data.
Are all cryptocurrency based on blockchain?
Most cryptocurrencies use blockchain technology to record transactions. For example, the bitcoin network and Ethereum network are both based on blockchain.
How long does it take to build a Dapp?
Dapps come in various sizes and extentions, on average it would take 2 months - 6 months but sometimes on rare cases it would take a year.
Is blockchain the future?
Blockchain is in its embryonic stage and has a lot of scope for evolution in the coming future; with the trend still undiscovered by many, one can find lesser competition in the field in terms of job opportunities.
What will replace blockchain?
Hedera, IOTA, Nano, and a few other ledgers are currently the only projects using acyclic graph technology. Still, we may see that change over time. DAGs are seen by many as an ideal replacement for blockchain, thanks to their enhanced data structure, increased throughput, ease of use, speed, and low fee structure.
Who owns the blockchain?
Summing up. To put it succinctly, nobody owns blockchain technology, and yet, everybody owns it. That is one of the unique features of blockchain tech, and this collective ownership and accountability is what makes blockchains very secure and immutable.
What is the biggest problem with blockchain?
Scalability The redundancy of blockchains makes them hard to scale. Every device in your network must have a copy of every transaction made, right from the genesis block to the most recent transaction. That means hundreds of copies of the same data!
How many Blockchains are there?
There are four main types of blockchain networks: public blockchains, private blockchains, consortium blockchains and hybrid blockchains. Each one of these platforms has its benefits, drawbacks and ideal uses
